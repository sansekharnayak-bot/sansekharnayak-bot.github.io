Biography & Timeline

1955: Born July 18 in Odisha, near the Brahmani river — a lifelong inspiration.
1970s: Composed first poems at school, blending science and literature.
1983: Debut collection published under Bhubaneswar Lekhak Samiti.
1980s–2019: 36-year academic career; prolific writer and literary critic.
2001: Odisha Sahitya Akademi Award for poetic excellence.
2018: Sarala Puraskar for “Misra Dhrupad,” blending tradition and modernity.
2024: Global release of English translation “WATER and Other Poems.”
2025: Manjari Devi Literature Award for lifelong literary contributions.

Major Poetry Collections

Misra Dhrupad

Award-winning 2018 collection blending traditional dhrupad with modern Odia themes: resistance, community, memory. Recognized with Sarala Puraskar.

Read More Download Listen

Misra Dhrupad – Details

Summary: “Misra Dhrupad” is Pandab's acclaimed collection fusing ancient musical forms (dhrupad) with contemporary Odia poetry. It explores social protest, dignity, and the memory of the marginalized.

Sample Poem:
“Every stanza becomes an act of remembrance and reimagination.
Echoes of rivers, salt marches, and rebellion interweave within each verse.”

Themes: Social justice, history, rural life, mythic and contemporary struggle.

Sabarmati

2005 Gandhi-themed collection, with 14 commentaries. Features “Gandhi Mahima Kavita,” “Dandi Das,” and “Sundo Gop Das.”

Read More Download Listen

Sabarmati – Details

Summary: “Sabarmati” reflects Gandhian ideals and Andhra's salt march, blending historical reflection and poetic commentary. Poems address ethics, peace, social change, and courage.

Sample Poem:
“Poetry walks with the Mahatma on the salt plains.
Forging unity from the river’s whisper and the broken chains.”

Themes & Impact

“Bhabi Nathi Keve / Bhoka ete niraba hoithai paari / Thuamula Ramapurara Manikeshwari nka / Andhara par niraba / Ete andhara je thakurani muh / Dishe nai din re bi.”
“Riding on a truck / Sanatana, where are you going? / To Therubhali or Damanjodi? / Wherever you go, there are hills. / And everywhere the hills are to be broken into dust...”
“Ask / the flustered women / blowing hard to keep the fire alight / as they push in the damp firewood, / what are they? / They can never answer / womanhood is a dazzling tongue of flame.”

Main Themes: Social resistance, caste, marginalization, emancipation, progressive change, empowerment, and the lived experience of the oppressed.

Awards & Recognition

Sarala Puraskar (2018)

Received for “Misra Dhrupad,” Satrughna Pandab was honored with Odisha's foremost literary award, celebrating his poetic voice and social impact. The Sarala Puraskar includes a cash prize and formal citation.

“This honor reflects both literary excellence and the voice of social change.”

Odisha Sahitya Akademi Award (2001)

Acknowledged for poetic excellence, likely linked to his poetry collection "Sabarmati" and other verses from 1998-2000.

Manjari Devi Literature Award (2025)

Honors his lifelong contributions to Odia literature from poetry to criticism and translation.
