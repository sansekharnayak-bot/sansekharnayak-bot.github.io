Biography

Satrughna Pandab (b. 19 July 1957) is an Odia poet whose voice marries resistance with tenderness. Below: a concise visual timeline of major life events and milestones.

1957 — Born

Born 19 July 1957 — early life, community roots and formative years.

1980s — First works

Early poetry collections that marked the voice of social justice and labor life.

2000 — Recognition

Established as a distinctive voice across Odisha; readings and academic interest grew.

2018 — Sarala Puraskar

Awarded the Sarala Puraskar for Misra Dhrupad, a major state literary honor.

2020 — Ongoing influence

Poems included in curricula and cited in contemporary literary criticism.

Themes & Impact

Major themes: social resistance, caste and marginalization, emancipation, and progressive change. The poetry often combines sharp social critique with humane, lyrical registers.

“Ask / the flustered women / blowing hard to keep the fire alight…” — celebrating the fierce flame of womanhood.

Awards & Recognition

Sarala Puraskar — 2018

Awarded for Misra Dhrupad. The Sarala Puraskar is a prestigious Odisha literary award presented by the Sarala Sahitya Sansad.
